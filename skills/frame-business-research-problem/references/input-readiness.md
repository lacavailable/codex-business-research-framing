# Input Readiness

Classify readiness before assessing fidelity. Missing information reduces the maturity ceiling; it does not authorize invention.

| Stage | Typical basis | Allowed output | Prohibited conclusion |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — exploratory framing | Topic or rough model idea; tentative actor and setting; incomplete decisions or constraints | Candidate decision structures, missing-information diagnosis, provisional scenarios, research-question candidates | Model fit, final academic claims, or unsupported empirical claims |
| 2 — model-grounded framing | Objective, decisions, constraints, timing, information, uncertainty, mechanism, and rough setting | DFC-12, gate audit, model mapping, eligible narrative, scenario comparison | Empirical importance not supported by evidence |
| 3 — evidence-grounded framing | Stage 2 plus credible institutional or empirical evidence and scope limits | Evidence-aware motivation, stronger significance claims, claim checklist, introduction draft | Causal or external-validity claims beyond the evidence |
| 4 — manuscript-ready framing | Model, results, contribution, literature position, evidence, audience, and manuscript text | Introduction, setting, implications, rewrite, and full framing audit | Publication guarantee or claims beyond the supplied analysis |

## Assign status

1. Record the stage supported by the supplied material, not the stage requested by the user.
2. List every missing item that blocks a higher stage.
3. Set each untestable consistency gate to `unknown` or `not_assessed`.
4. Set `eligibility_status: not_assessed` unless all gates can be tested.
5. Use `ineligible` only when at least one gate demonstrably fails.
6. Use `eligible` only when all six gates pass.

Treat Stage 2 as the primary intended use. Formal equations are strongly recommended but not mandatory when the conceptual model precisely identifies roles, timing, information, mechanism, objective, and constraints.
