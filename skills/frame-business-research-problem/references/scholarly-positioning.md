# Scholarly Positioning

Apply this layer after fidelity and alongside managerial framing when the requested mode concerns a paper, research question, introduction, or contribution.

## Position the opportunity

1. Define the phenomenon or research territory narrowly.
2. Summarize what the supplied literature establishes; do not fabricate a literature consensus.
3. Identify a limitation, anomaly, tension, or challenged assumption.
4. Convert that issue into a bounded research opportunity and explicit research question.
5. Name the contribution type: theoretical, methodological, computational, empirical, or integrative.
6. Explain novelty relative to prior work, not merely novelty of context.
7. Separate theoretical or technical value from practical utility.
8. State boundaries and counterconditions that limit the contribution.

## Diagnose weak positioning

Treat these as insufficient on their own:

- “little research has studied” without explaining why the omission matters;
- a new dataset, geography, technology, or industry without a changed mechanism or inference;
- a list of paper sections presented as contributions;
- a practical claim that has no operational pathway from the result to a decision;
- problematization that misstates the model or prior literature.

When literature is incomplete, label the positioning provisional and provide a targeted verification checklist rather than asserting novelty.
