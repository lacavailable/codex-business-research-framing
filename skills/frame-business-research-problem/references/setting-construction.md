# Business-Setting Construction

When creating or improving a business setting, generate up to three candidate
interpretations internally. Assess each candidate on actor control, decision
timing, information availability, objective alignment, constraint plausibility,
behavioral-mechanism fit, managerial significance, evidence accessibility,
contribution fit, and boundary transparency.

Classify each candidate:

- `direct_interpretation`: the supplied model directly represents the setting;
- `plausible_application`: the mapping is credible but requires identified
  contextual evidence;
- `illustrative_analogy`: the setting explains structure but is not asserted as
  a direct application;
- `model_extension`: the setting requires a new actor, decision, information,
  behavior, constraint, objective, or mechanism.

Discard ineligible direct/application claims. Rank eligible candidates by the
unchanged noncompensatory order: model fit, managerial significance, structural
tension, evidence plausibility, contribution potential, and boundary
transparency.

By default, show only the strongest eligible framing and its most important
boundary. Show alternatives and rejected candidates only when the user asks for
comparison or brainstorming. Never present an analogy as a direct
interpretation.
