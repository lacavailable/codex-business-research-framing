# Introduction Architecture

Use a flexible sequence; do not force every item into a separate paragraph or imitate a publisher's house style.

1. Open with a consequential decision or phenomenon.
2. Establish stakes with supplied evidence or a labeled evidence need.
3. Explain the mechanism or structural tension.
4. Identify a limitation in current understanding.
5. Define the resulting research opportunity.
6. State the research question.
7. Introduce the method or model at the level needed to understand the answer.
8. State the bounded contribution.
9. Explain relevance without extending beyond the model and evidence.

Audit each externally checkable, causal, prevalence, trend, and impact claim. Avoid generic trend openings and unsupported “increasingly important” language. A CARS-like territory–niche–response movement may help organize the introduction, but rhetoric never establishes empirical importance or fidelity.
