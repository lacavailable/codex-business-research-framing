# Limitations

- The framework cannot establish whether an empirical claim is true. It identifies claims needing evidence and depends on the sources supplied to it.
- A model can omit an operationally important actor, behavior, constraint, or outcome. Faithful framing exposes that boundary but does not repair the model.
- Scenario ranking is conditional on the provided model and evidence. It does not establish external validity or commercial importance.
- Synthetic benchmark cases cannot represent every OM, IS, or OR research design, institutional setting, culture, or regulatory environment.
- Language-model outputs and model-based judgments are nondeterministic and may change across model versions. Same-model generation and judging can create correlated blind spots.
- Blinding condition labels reduces but does not eliminate contamination: wording patterns may reveal the condition, and a model may have prior familiarity with common framing concepts.
- A stronger formulation or faster algorithm has managerial value only through an explicit decision pathway, such as returning a better incumbent or certificate before a deadline.
- The Skill does not provide legal, ethical, security, statistical, or domain-expert review. Human authors remain responsible for claims, citations, model interpretation, and disclosure.
