# Prestige and recognition bias diagnostics

These diagnostics are separate from production evaluation. Identical content is randomized across hidden versus revealed journal identity, “published” versus “working paper” labels, original versus expert-verified faithful paraphrase, headings added versus removed, and length increased without information.

Fresh blinded judges receive randomized arm order. Production packets never expose prestige. The preregistered prestige gate requires an absolute mean label effect below two normalized points and a 95% confidence-interval upper bound below three. Paraphrase diagnostics require a median absolute difference no greater than five, rank correlation of at least 0.80, and at least 90% hard-failure agreement. Verbosity should reduce clarity while changing fidelity and evidence by no more than one raw point on average.

These tests are blocked until lawful passages, expert-verified paraphrases, and fresh judge-family attestations exist. Synthetic fixtures validate randomization and analysis code but cannot answer recognition bias empirically.
