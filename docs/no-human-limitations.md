# No-human limitations

_Limits on interpretation of automated source-grounded triangulation_

---

## Central limitation

No qualified human OM, IS, OR, or management reviewers participate in the automated pathway. Automated role separation can expose internal disagreement and test designed contrasts, but it cannot establish what domain scholars would judge correct, important, publishable, or useful.

## Validity boundaries

- **Silver is not gold.** Confidence-graded silver labels are automated, source-grounded decisions, not expert annotations.
- **Source grounding is not expert review.** Private passages constrain interpretations but do not make the evaluator a domain expert.
- **Outlet inclusion is not a quality label.** A passage is assessed on its content and context, never presumed strong because of journal prestige.
- **Same-family diversity is limited.** Different model variants, prompts, ordering, and contexts may share training, architecture, and systematic errors.
- **Agreement can be correlated.** Multiple automated roles can repeat the same misconception; numerical agreement is not independent corroboration.
- **Synthetic controls are designed.** They measure recovery of preregistered constructs and perturbations, not the full distribution of real scholarly writing.
- **Private coverage is narrow.** Seventeen retained sources and 24 candidate notes cannot represent all domains, methods, passage functions, outlets, or writing styles.
- **Context reconstruction is lossy.** Focused passages and context packets can omit dependencies that matter to an article's full argument.
- **Copyright limits auditability.** Public artifacts exclude source text and evidence spans, so outside reviewers can verify hashes and aggregate logic but not reproduce private judgments without lawful access.
- **Holdout evidence is conditional.** A one-time automated holdout estimates performance on its frozen construction, not future external validity.
- **Scores are framework-dependent.** Category weights, applicability, materiality, and thresholds encode project choices.
- **Experimental performance is task-bounded.** A benchmark result applies to the included modes, domains, response profiles, prompts, and models.

## Bias diagnostics

Prestige, publication-status, paraphrase, heading, and verbosity diagnostics test specific sources of evaluator bias. Passing them does not prove absence of recognition bias, stylistic preference, memorization, or contamination. Failed diagnostics block the automated release gate; successful diagnostics only remove those preregistered blockers.

## Governance consequence

Tier A may authorize only an experimental prerelease. Release materials must state that no human experts participated, source passages served as private anchors, automated roles are not experts, annotations are silver labels, and future human validation remains necessary.

Tier B remains the only path for formal expert-validation claims. Nothing in the automated study changes its reviewer, holdout, or evidence requirements.
