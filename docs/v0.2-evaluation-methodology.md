# v0.2 Evaluation Methodology

## Design

The forward benchmark contains 42 synthetic cases, balanced at 14 each for OM, IS, and OR. It preserves the original 30 cases and adds 12 harder cases, eight labeled adversarial. Each case is generated under three conditions:

1. a normal no-Skill user request;
2. the archived `v0.1.0-alpha` Skill materialized from its Git tag in ignored evaluation storage;
3. the current `v0.2.0-beta` Skill.

This produces 126 outputs. Baselines receive serialized case material only; only their designated Skill condition receives a Skill path.

## Blinding and judging

Outputs are assigned randomized blind identifiers with condition labels withheld. Two fresh-context, condition-blind judges score every output, yielding 252 primary score records. A third adjudicator is used only when judges disagree about a hard failure or differ by more than 20 percentage points on a dimension.

The canonical rubric scores fidelity (30), managerial framing (25), scholarly positioning (20), evidence discipline (15), and prose clarity (10). Success requires at least 70 points, every applicable layer minimum, and no fidelity hard failure. Raw scores are preserved; hard-failure totals are capped at 69.

## Reported analyses

Report paired and category deltas, hard-failure and refusal rates, regressions, verbosity, ceiling effects, structural preference bias, mean absolute judge differences, total-score correlation, and hard-failure agreement. Highlight cases where a condition refuses a commercially appealing but inconsistent story.

## Reproducibility and limitations

Prompts, outputs, blind mappings, and scores are committed as individual schema-valid JSON files. Language-model judging is intentionally outside required CI; CI checks artifact completeness and report integrity. Same-model judging can create shared preferences, and condition wording or structural output requirements can remain detectable despite blinding. These residual contamination and preference risks must accompany all conclusions.
