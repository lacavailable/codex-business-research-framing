# Literature Synthesis

## Scope and evidentiary boundary

This synthesis distills the fixed 20-source v0.2 corpus into original operational principles. Read it with the canonical [manifest](../literature/manifest.yaml) and audited cards: sources were accessible only as metadata, an official abstract, a publisher summary, or official guidance. A card supports no more than its recorded access scope. No source text is redistributed.

## Convergent findings

### Consequential decisions need accountable structure

The audited HBR contributor guidance supports writing for a defined audience with clear relevance. The Skill combines that bounded outlet guidance with the original DFC-12 design: named actor, trigger, controlled choice, decision time, information set, stakes, and constraints. The corpus does not establish that any source prescribed those twelve fields or the six noncompensatory gates.

### Mechanism and tension make a problem nontrivial

The current audited access scope does not substantiate a literature-derived mechanism-and-tension rule. The Skill retains the pathway from choice to outcome and the nontrivial trade-off as original DFC-12 requirements. When a mechanism is proposed rather than established, it remains an assumption or inference.

### Importance requires evidence discipline

Official outlet guidance supports making significance and usefulness visible, but neither confident prose nor publisher placement establishes magnitude, prevalence, or causality. The Skill labels empirical facts, model assumptions, simplifying assumptions, inferences, and unsupported claims. Practitioner exemplars are bounded observations of execution only.

### Contribution is a bounded claim

The audited Corley-Gioia and Locke-Golden-Biddle abstracts jointly support describing contribution relative to prior literature and identifying its claimed originality or utility without treating rhetoric as proof. The Alvesson-Sandberg abstract separately supports assumption-challenging problematization as one possible route to a research question, not a guaranteed recipe for influence.

### Rhetorical architecture is subordinate to validity

The audited Swales record is bibliographic metadata only, so it does not substantiate a CARS-derived Skill rule. The flexible territory-opportunity-response sequence remains an original writing aid. It cannot establish empirical importance, novelty, or model fit and must not become a publisher imitation.

### Analytical performance needs an operational pathway

The Landry-Malouin-Oral abstract motivates attention to model validation but does not establish the Skill's decision-deadline pathway. The rule that runtime, bounds, or certificates create managerial value only when they change an available action in time is an original framework rule. Realized profit or implementation success requires separate evidence.

## Domain implications

- **OM:** distinguish display, inventory, production, and fulfillment capacity; make substitution, congestion, or service mechanisms explicit; connect relevance to the actual actor and process.
- **IS:** distinguish latent types from observed signals and private platform objectives from welfare, fairness, privacy, or safety outcomes.
- **OR:** preserve feasible sets, objectives, guarantees, deadlines, and the pathway between computational output and organizational action.

These domain rules are original framework rules unless the generated source-to-rule matrix explicitly lists admitted literature support.

## Exclusions and unresolved limits

Additional exemplars and editorials were excluded when they did not materially change a rule or would overconcentrate one outlet. Access-limited cards constrain substantive interpretation. The corpus is focused rather than systematic and is not a substitute for the literature review required by a specific paper. See the [selection log](source-selection-and-exclusion-log.md), [design document](literature-grounded-skill-design.md), and generated [source-to-rule matrix](../skills/frame-business-research-problem/references/source-to-rule-matrix.md).
