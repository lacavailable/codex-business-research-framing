# Product-owner development review 04

**Task:** `IS-S02`

## Request

Diagnose and repair this model interpretation. Users have latent benign or malicious types. The platform observes a noisy signal, chooses which accounts receive review, and then takes a moderation action. The draft treats true type as observed at allocation time and calls platform revenue social welfare. Produce a faithful, usable framing and identify the essential evidence need and principal boundary.

## Output A

## Diagnosis

The draft is **ineligible as written** for two fidelity failures:

- **Information:** latent user type cannot be treated as observed when review is allocated. The platform can condition that allocation only on the noisy signal (and any other explicitly observed variables).
- **Objective:** platform revenue is a private objective, not social welfare. Welfare would additionally require defined and evaluated effects on benign users, malicious users, and affected third parties.

## Repaired framing

A platform must allocate limited account-review capacity before knowing whether any individual account is benign or malicious. For each account, it observes a noisy risk signal, selects which accounts to review, receives whatever review information the model specifies, and then chooses a moderation action. The platform’s objective is to maximize its modeled expected revenue (or platform payoff), accounting for the consequences of review and moderation under the model’s stated mechanism.

The structural tension is clear: prioritizing accounts with higher estimated risk can direct scarce review effort toward likely harmful activity, but signal error can cause benign accounts to be reviewed or moderated while malicious accounts evade review. The model evaluates the platform’s private payoff under this screening-and-action policy; it does not establish that the policy maximizes welfare or safety.

## Decision mapping

| Model object | Business meaning | Decision-time status |
|---|---|---|
| True type | Whether an account is benign or malicious | Latent at review-allocation time |
| Noisy signal | Automated risk score or other pre-review indicator | Observed before allocation |
| Review allocation | Accounts selected for scarce review | Platform decision |
| Review result | Information generated by review | Observed only after selected review, as specified |
| Moderation action | Warn, restrict, remove, or other modeled action | Platform decision after the stated information arrives |
| Revenue objective | Platform’s modeled monetary payoff | Objective, not social welfare |

## Essential evidence need

Obtain data linking **pre-review signals, review findings, moderation actions, and platform revenue consequences**. This is needed to calibrate signal error and the payoff mechanism rather than treating either as an empirical fact.

## Principal boundary

The analysis supports conclusions about the platform’s revenue-maximizing review-and-moderation policy under the modeled information structure. It does **not** establish effects on social welfare, fairness, user rights, safety, or broader harms unless those outcomes are explicitly modeled and evaluated.

## Output B

### Replacement framing

A platform allocates account reviews under imperfect information. Each account’s benign or malicious type is latent; at allocation time, the platform observes only a noisy signal and selects accounts for review using that signal. It then takes a moderation action using the information available after review, as specified by the model. The platform’s modeled objective is expected platform revenue—not social welfare. The research question is: how should the platform map noisy signals into review allocation and subsequent moderation actions to maximize expected revenue, subject to the model’s review-feasibility constraints?

| Model object | Business meaning | Decision-time status |
|---|---|---|
| True user type | Benign or malicious account characteristic | Latent at review allocation |
| Noisy signal | Imperfect risk indicator available to the platform | Observed before allocation |
| Review allocation | Accounts selected for review | Platform decision |
| Moderation action | Enforcement decision after review | Platform decision; review information is not supplied |
| Revenue | Platform’s private payoff | Objective, not welfare |

### Diagnosis

The draft is **contradicted** on two material points:

- It leaks information by treating true type as observed when review is allocated; allocation may condition on the noisy signal, not latent type.
- It inflates the objective by calling platform revenue social welfare. Revenue excludes unmodeled harms to benign users, malicious-user harms, fairness, safety, and other externalities.

### Essential evidence need

Evidence is needed that the proposed signal is meaningfully related to account type in the intended setting, including its error properties and the information produced by review.

### Principal boundary

This is a revenue-focused platform-governance model. It does not establish welfare, fairness, or safety improvement unless those outcomes are explicitly modeled and evaluated.
